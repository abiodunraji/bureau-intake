Answer · part one

Three answers, one page.

The v1 build plan answered "how" but never answered "why." Here are the three answers in their shortest form. The detail behind each one lives in the strategic plan v2 that accompanies this document.

ROI

€70k–€150k additional ARR in year one.

Against operating cost of roughly €1,800 per year. Payback in weeks, not years. The build itself is mostly agent work, not paid headcount.

The goal

Stop selling a service. Sell a number.

A 0-to-100 score that becomes the unit of conversation in the Dutch physio market, and that only Bureau Intake can calculate.

Reason to live

Own the standard, or compete on price forever.

Generic SEO is a commodity. Whoever owns the measurement standard owns the category. Build it now, become the reference. Wait, and somebody else will.

Where the ARR delta comes from

Five operational levers move when "we offer SEO" becomes "see your number." They compound, they do not stack linearly. The table is calibrated with industry averages for the current state — Iwan's actual numbers (see page 3) sharpen these into a real forecast.

Lever Today After launch Why it moves
Inbound leads / month ~0 10–20 Free score lookup is an irresistible self-diagnostic. Anyone scoring below 60 enters the funnel.
Close rate on contact 10–15% 30–40% Prospect arrives knowing her problem and trusting your methodology. The diagnosis came from you.
Monthly churn 10–15% 3–5% Clients watch the number climb. Cancelling means watching it fall. Hard to do.
Avg revenue / client €590 €590–€890 A Premium tier with full dashboard, benchmarking, AI-mention reports becomes defensible at €890.
Net clients at m12 8–12 20–35 Compound of all four levers above.
ARR at m12 €55–85k €140–275k Same business, different shape.
Payback math. Year-one operating cost is around €1,800. Year-one ARR delta is between €55k and €190k depending on which end of the range plays out. That is between 30x and 100x return on operating cost — and the cost itself is bounded, while the upside compounds in years two and three.
Answer · part two

The bigger value is not on the napkin.

The lever math on page 1 is the direct ARR case. Four second-order effects sit underneath it that are not in the table — and that, together, are worth more than the direct math.

You own the standard

Once 500+ Dutch practices have looked themselves up on /intake-score, "Intake Score" becomes the language. Competitor agencies have to reference your number to be taken seriously. You stop being a vendor; you become the measurement layer.

Annual report = a year of inbound

"The State of Visibility in Dutch Physiotherapy 2027" becomes a press piece, a citable SEO asset, and a sales tool. Cost to produce: ~40 hours. Value: inbound interest for the next twelve months.

Exit multiple changes shape

A services agency at €100k ARR sells for 1–2x ARR if it sells at all. A specialised SaaS-adjacent business with proprietary data at €250k ARR sells for 4–8x ARR. Same person, same effort, four to ten times the eventual exit value.

Exit the price comparison

Today, anyone selling local SEO at €390 undercuts you. After the score exists, you are not in that comparison. You are selling something they do not have. The price floor moves up structurally.

The reason to live, in one paragraph

If Iwan does nothing, in 24 months Bureau Intake is roughly 15-25 clients, MRR €9-15k, indistinguishable from twenty other small Dutch SEO shops, fully dependent on cold-call output, growing linearly with how many hours one person can spend on the phone. If Iwan builds this, in 24 months Bureau Intake owns the metric the Dutch physio industry uses to measure itself, has 50+ clients on a higher tier, generates the majority of new leads inbound, and is a credible acquisition target for adjacent verticals (dental marketing groups, healthcare martech, Dutch wellness platforms).

The reason this needs to live. Without it, Bureau Intake is a job for one person, scaling linearly with that person's hours, with no compounding asset and no defensible position in the market. With it, Bureau Intake becomes the company everyone references when they talk about physio local visibility in the Netherlands — and the work continues even when Iwan steps away from the phone.

Answer · part three

Three numbers from Iwan would replace assumptions with forecast.

The math on page 1 uses industry-average assumptions for Bureau Intake's current state. Three of Iwan's actual numbers would turn that table from a model into a forecast.

Number What it sharpens Why
Current paying clients and MRR Anchors the "today" column. Determines whether m12 base case is €140k or €270k ARR. The model assumes 5 clients / €35k baseline. If Iwan is at 12, year-one outcomes scale up proportionally.
Last-90-days cold-call conversion rate Anchors the "today" close-rate row. Defines how much room there is for the inbound funnel to outperform. If the current rate is already 25%, the gain from warm leads is smaller; if it is 5%, the gain is much larger.
Monthly churn (or avg client lifetime) Anchors the retention assumption. Drives both year-one and year-two ARR projections. The score-as-KPI effect on retention is the single biggest indirect lever. Knowing today's number gives a real measurement.
