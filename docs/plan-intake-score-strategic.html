01 — Executive summary

The bet, in one page.

Bureau Intake sells local SEO services to physiotherapy practices. This plan replaces "selling a service" with "selling a number" — a 0 to 100 score that becomes the unit of conversation in the Dutch physio market, and that only Bureau Intake can calculate.

What we are actually building

A daily-updated 0 to 100 score for every physiotherapy practice in the Netherlands, computed from five proprietary signals. Five components:

  • Public lookup at intake-score — any physiotherapist types her practice name, sees her score and the three areas dragging it down. The form captures her email. She becomes a lead.
  • Daily data collection — five Cloudflare Workers crawl SERPs, Google Bedrijfsprofielen, AI assistants, and 20 Dutch healthcare directories. No human touches the data.
  • Client dashboard at portal — paying clients log in, watch their score climb, see what drives each signal.
  • Admin at admin — Iwan adds practices, pauses them, and reads the error log. That is the whole operating surface.
  • Annual report — once the dataset is large, "The State of Visibility in Dutch Physiotherapy 2027" becomes the inbound funnel for year two.

Why this works

The defensible asset is not the score formula. The defensible asset is the dataset that powers it. Every day Bureau Intake collects daily SERP positions, AI-mention rates, and review velocity for thousands of Dutch physio practices. A competitor starting six months from now is six months behind on accuracy, and the gap widens, not closes. Twelve months in, the historical data alone is worth more than a year of cold-calling could ever produce.

One-paragraph version. Bureau Intake stops being one of dozens of Dutch SEO agencies competing on price and starts being the company that owns the measurement standard for physio local visibility. The financial case (page 4), product case (page 7), and risk case (page 16) all reduce to that single move.
02 — Why we are building this

The case for the spend.

The honest version of this argument starts with the problem the current business cannot solve, then the math that changes once it can, then the risk of doing nothing.

What the current business cannot do

Linear growth ceiling

Cold-call sales scale with hours on the phone. There is no leverage. Twenty hours of calls a week is a ceiling, not a starting point.

No proprietary asset

Today, Bureau Intake's value is in Iwan's head and Iwan's calendar. If a competitor copies the website, the messaging, and the price, there is no remaining defensible layer.

Vague deliverable

"We will improve your local SEO" is hard to price, hard to retain on, and hard to defend against a competitor pitching the same at €390.

No inbound funnel

Every euro of revenue today required a phone call that started cold. No multiplier, no compounding, no break.

What changes once the score exists

A free public lookup converts "we offer SEO" into "see your number." Anyone with a practice can self-diagnose in 30 seconds. The conversation is no longer about whether to trust Bureau Intake. It is about how to get the number up. That is a different sale.

Five operational levers move once that shift happens. They compound, they do not stack linearly.

02 — Why we are building this

Second-order effects, and the cost of doing nothing.

The lever math on page 4 covers the direct ARR case. The bigger value sits in the second-order effects, and the strategic risk sits in the counterfactual. Both are worth more than the direct math suggests.

Second-order effects (not in the lever math)

You own the standard

Once 500+ Dutch practices have looked themselves up, "Intake Score" enters their vocabulary. Trade press cites it. Competitor agencies have to reference it to be taken seriously. You stop being a vendor; you become the measurement layer.

Annual report is inbound for the next year

Year-two "State of Visibility" report becomes a press piece, an SEO asset (other physio blogs link to it), and a sales tool ("according to our 2027 data, practices that fix NAP first see X"). Cost to produce: ~40 hours. Value: a year of inbound interest.

Exit multiple changes shape

Services agency at €100k ARR sells for 1–2x ARR if it sells at all. Specialised SaaS-adjacent business with proprietary data at €250k ARR sells for 4–8x ARR. Same person, same effort, four to ten times the eventual exit value.

You exit the price comparison

Right now anyone selling "local SEO for €390" is your competitor. After the score exists, you are not in that comparison. You are selling something they do not have. Price floor moves up structurally.

24 months 15–25 clients, MRR €9–15k Iwan-time is the bottleneck. Cannot meaningfully scale beyond what one person can sell.

03 — The product

Three user experiences, one underlying engine.

De Intake Score is one continuously-running engine that surfaces three distinct views. Each view exists for a different person, with a different goal, but they share the same data and the same daily refresh.

For the practice owner

Visits intake-score, types her practice name. Sees her score, her peer percentile, the three weakest signals, and a one-paragraph diagnosis. Leaves her email to see "how to improve." Becomes a lead in the sales pipeline.

For the paying client

Logs into the portal via email magic link. Sees a history graph of her score climbing. Drills into each signal. Sees auto-generated tasks for the month. Forwards progress to her partner or accountant.

For Iwan

Logs into the admin. Adds new practices by pasting GBP URLs. Pauses clients who churn. Reads the error log if a parser breaks. Never enters data, never tags reviews by hand, never reads search results manually.

What sets the score apart

Versus generic SEO tools Versus competing local SEO agencies
Physio-specific. Generic tools score against generic-business benchmarks. This benchmarks against other Dutch physios in the same postcode. The competitor has no benchmark. Bureau Intake has 12 months of physio-specific Dutch data and the only AI-mention tracker in the country.
Includes the AI-mention rate. No generic SEO tool tracks ChatGPT/Claude/Perplexity mentions. Bureau Intake does, daily. The competitor's deliverable is a Word doc and a phone call. Bureau Intake's deliverable is a number that updates every morning at 06:00.
Live. The score recalculates daily; recommendations adapt automatically as signals shift. The competitor cannot show monthly progress because she has no measurement instrument. Renewals become harder for her every quarter.
What the product is not. Not a CRM. Not an analytics dashboard. Not a Google rank tracker. It does one thing: produce a defensible, proprietary number that summarises a Dutch physiotherapy practice's local visibility, and then walks the practice owner through what to fix next.
04 — The five signals

What feeds the number.

Five independent inputs, each collected and parsed automatically, combined into the 0 to 100 score with a weighting Iwan can tune over time. Signal five is the moat.

# Signal Weight What it measures and why it is defensible
1 GBP completeness 20% Photos, hours, posts, Q&A, attributes, behandelingen, recency of profile updates. Easy to score; useful only in combination with the rest.
2 Local SERP position 30% Daily rank for "fysio [stad]", "[behandeling] [stad]" across every postcode the practice serves. The percentile score requires 12+ months of history to be meaningful — that history is the moat.
3 Review velocity 20% Reviews per month over trailing 90 days, average rating, sentiment of recent text. LLM sentiment uses a physio-specific lexicon you refine over time.
4 NAP consistency 10% Naam, Adres, Phone across ~20 Dutch healthcare directories. Maintaining the 20 scrapers is significant ongoing work that nobody else is doing.
5 AI-mention rate moat 20% Daily queries to ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, Gemini asking "best fysio in [stad] voor [klacht]". LLM extracts which practices each AI mentions and the sentiment. Nobody in the Netherlands is systematically tracking this. Whoever starts first wins the next two years.

How the weights get tuned

Starting weights are educated guesses. Once 90 days of data is in, Iwan can correlate "score change" against "real-world client outcomes" (new patient appointments, calls received, agenda fill) and adjust. The admin panel has a slider per signal. Reweighting is a 30-second change, applied across the entire dataset.

Why not just track Google rank? Because Google rank moves slowly, swings randomly, and ignores everything that happens off-Google. The score is designed to move based on what Bureau Intake's clients can actually control, on a timeline that matches the monthly work cycle.
05 — System architecture

Cloudflare-native. No DevOps. Near-zero ops.

Everything runs on the Cloudflare account that already hosts bureauintake.nl. No new infrastructure to manage. No separate hosting bills. No on-call rotation. The system is observable from a single dashboard.

Tech choices and why

Layer Choice Why
Compute Cloudflare Workers (cron + HTTP) Free tier covers our daily volume. Same account as the site. Cron triggers built in.
Database Cloudflare D1 (managed SQLite) Free up to 5GB. Zero ops. Single-region fine — not latency sensitive.
Queues Cloudflare Queues Rate-limits SERP and LLM calls. Avoids upstream-API bans.
Auth Cloudflare Access (magic links) No password to manage. Iwan logs in via email.
Email Resend 100 emails/day free. Alerts + weekly reports.
Frontend Astro (same repo) New routes /intake-score, /portal, /admin alongside existing pages.
05 — System architecture (continued)

Data handling, security, observability.

What gets stored, who can see it, and how the system tells Iwan when something is wrong.

What we store, and what we do not

What we store

  • Practice name, address, phone, GBP URL (all public information)
  • Daily SERP positions for tracked queries
  • GBP completeness snapshot, review count, average rating
  • Sentiment scores derived from public review text
  • AI-assistant responses to physio queries
  • Computed daily score + signal-level breakdown

What we do not store

  • Full text of patient reviews (only sentiment scores and excerpts shown to the practice owner)
  • Personal data about patients (we never see it)
  • Anything the practice itself has not made public
  • Credit card or financial information (handled by the payment provider, never touches our database)
  • Email addresses for non-clients beyond what they themselves submitted to the public form

GDPR posture

All data we collect is already publicly available. Practice information comes from Google Bedrijfsprofielen and healthcare directories. SERP positions come from public Google results. AI-assistant responses are reactions to public queries. No patient data is in scope. The legal basis for processing public business data of physiotherapy practices is the legitimate interest of providing them with a visibility analysis, and we offer a clear opt-out path from our admin.

Observability

Surface Purpose
Cloudflare Workers dashboard Per-Worker execution log, error rate, average duration. Bureau Intake sees this without configuration.
D1 query metrics Storage usage, read/write counts. Free tier limits become visible long before they are hit.
Error log (/admin) Plain-language list of any parser failures, API rejections, score-calculation anomalies. Iwan reads this weekly.
Discord webhook Real-time alerts for score drops, new sub-3-star reviews, system errors. Optional, configurable per client.
Failure mode design. Every external API call is wrapped in retry-with-backoff. If a directory's HTML changes and the parser breaks, the system marks that signal as "stale" for that practice and lowers its weight in the score temporarily. The score never silently lies. Iwan sees the stale-signal flag in /admin within 24 hours.
06 — Operating model

What runs alone. What Iwan touches.

The system collects, scores, alerts, and reports on its own every day. Iwan's involvement is intentionally small. Three buttons in the admin and an inbox for alerts.

The daily run

Worker Cadence Behaviour
serp-collector Daily 03:00 Queries DataForSEO for "fysio [stad]" plus behandelingen across each tracked postcode. Stores top-10 results.
gbp-scanner Daily 03:30 Fetches Google Places data per practice. Scores completeness, computes review velocity, runs LLM sentiment on the latest 10 reviews.
ai-mention-collector Daily 04:00 Asks ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, Gemini physio queries per city. An LLM parses each answer and logs every practice name that appears, with sentiment.
nap-checker Weekly Sun 02:00 Visits 20 healthcare directories per practice. LLM normalises name, address, phone. Flags divergence from canonical GBP.
score-calculator Daily 06:00 Reads all signals, applies weighted formula, writes daily snapshot, computes peer percentile.
alerter Daily 06:30 Detects score drops >5pts, new sub-3★ reviews, competitor entries to top-3, AI-mention disappearances. Emails Iwan + Discord webhook.

What Iwan actually touches

  • Add a practice Paste a Google Bedrijfsprofiel URL. The system pulls everything itself, starts monitoring, returns the first score within 24 hours.
  • Pause or resume a practice Toggle off when a client churns, on when they return. Historical data is preserved.
  • Read the error log Only populates when a parser breaks. Each entry is a clear "this needs a fix when you have a moment." Never urgent, never silent.
  • Tune weights (rarely) Sliders in /admin for the five signal weights. Changes apply retroactively across the whole dataset. Iwan touches this maybe twice in year one.
The total operating surface. No spreadsheets. No manual review tagging. No "let me check what Perplexity said today." No NAP variations counted by hand. Every signal is collected, parsed, normalised, and scored without a human ever entering or judging data. Iwan reads alerts and adds clients. Everything else is on cron.

08 — Go-to-market

How the product becomes the channel.

The product is also the marketing. The free public lookup is the funnel. The dashboard is the retention. The annual report is the press. None of these are separate spend lines — they are emergent from the product existing.

Phase A — Pre-launch (weeks 1–8 of build)

  • Silent data collection. The Workers start running before any public surface exists. By the time /intake-score goes live, the system already has 8 weeks of signal history for every practice in scope.
  • Soft launch with existing clients. Iwan adds his current paying clients first. They get private access to /portal and see their scores. Their feedback shapes the recommendation copy.
  • Seed cities. Pick 5 Dutch cities (Amsterdam, Utrecht, Den Haag, Rotterdam, Alkmaar) and track every physio practice in each. By launch day, scores exist for ~150 practices Bureau Intake has never spoken to.

Phase B — Launch (week 10)

  • /intake-score goes public. Anyone can look up any tracked practice's score. Practice owners who score under 60 see a "fix this in 90 days" offer with email capture.
  • Outreach to seeded practices. The 150 already-tracked practices receive a short cold email: "we have analysed your visibility, here is your free report." Conversion much higher than a generic cold call because the report is concrete.
  • Single Dutch trade journal partnership. Submit one piece to ParaMedisch or Fysio Magazine explaining the score methodology. Free distribution, instant credibility.

Phase C — Year-one growth (months 4–12)

Channel Mechanic Expected contribution
Free lookup tool Practice owner self-diagnoses, leaves email, enters sales pipeline ~50% of new leads
SEO content Blog posts that rank for "lokale SEO fysiotherapie" link to /intake-score ~15% of new leads
Trade press / referrals Existing clients refer peers, trade journals cite the methodology ~20% of new leads
Cold outreach (warmed) Iwan still calls, but with a personalised score report in hand. Conversion much higher than today. ~15% of new leads
The role of cold calling changes, but does not disappear. Today cold calling is the only channel and the conversion is low. After launch, cold calling is one of four channels, but every call starts with a personalised, data-backed report. Calls per closed client drops 4–5x. Iwan still calls — he just closes more of them.
08 — Go-to-market (continued)

Pricing strategy.

Today Bureau Intake has one tier at €590/month. After the score launches, there is a path to a three-tier structure that captures more value from clients who want depth without scaring off the budget-conscious ones who do not.

Tier Price Includes Target
Free €0 One-time score lookup at /intake-score. Three weakest signals. Email capture for "how to improve." Lead funnel. Anyone in NL with a practice.
Standard €590/mo Current Bureau Intake service plus client dashboard at /portal. Daily score updates. Monthly auto-generated tasks. Email alerts. Single-location practices wanting full coverage of the basics.
Premium €890/mo Everything in Standard plus quarterly competitor analysis, monthly AI-mention report, custom annual report for the practice, priority response on alerts. Larger practices, multi-location chains, practices with a marketing budget.

How the tiering rolls out

Existing clients stay on €590 grandfathered. New clients post-launch are offered Standard or Premium. After 6 months of Premium running, Standard clients receive a one-time upgrade offer with two-month Premium trial. Conservative assumption: 25–35% of clients eventually move to Premium. That alone adds 12–18% to average revenue per client.

10 — Risks and mitigations

What can go wrong, and what we do about it.

The honest list. Most of these are mitigated by the architecture or the phasing. None of them is a project-killer on its own. The combination of two or three at once is what matters — addressed in the contingency section below.

Risk Likelihood Impact and mitigation
Upstream API price changes Medium DataForSEO or LLM providers raise prices, doubling costs. Mitigation: the architecture supports swapping providers with one config change. Multiple LLM providers already in rotation.
Directory HTML changes High Healthcare directories occasionally change their pages, breaking NAP scrapers. Mitigation: parsers degrade gracefully, signal marked stale rather than missing. Fix is a 30-minute job per parser; expected 2–4 times/year total.
Google policy on Places API Low Google changes Maps Platform pricing or terms. Mitigation: GBP data is one signal of five; the score can absorb a 20% increase or partial loss of this signal without breaking.
Slow product-market fit Medium Practices ignore the free lookup, conversion rate underperforms. Mitigation: each phase is independently valuable. Even with poor inbound conversion, the dashboard improves client retention enough to justify the build.
Competitor copies the idea Low Another agency notices and builds something similar. Mitigation: 12 months of historical data is the moat. They start at month one; you are at month 13. The gap widens.
Iwan unavailable mid-build Low Vacation, illness, business event takes Iwan out of the loop. Mitigation: agent work continues against the existing plan. Decision points are scheduled, not constant — most weeks need ~30 minutes of Iwan's input.
GDPR complaint from a tracked practice Low A practice owner objects to being tracked without consent. Mitigation: a public opt-out form removes a practice within 24 hours. Architecture supports this from day one. All data is already public.
AI assistants change behaviour Medium ChatGPT/Claude/Perplexity change how they return local recommendations, devaluing the AI-mention signal. Mitigation: signal weights are tunable. If one AI becomes unreliable, drop it; the other three remain.
What would actually kill this project. Two scenarios. First: Google launches its own "physio visibility score" inside Google Bedrijfsprofiel before Bureau Intake's tool reaches critical mass — unlikely (Google has not built vertical-specific scoring in any of its other markets). Second: Iwan loses motivation to operate the system after launch — entirely a human factor, not a technical one. Mitigated by keeping operations to 15 minutes/week and by the inbound funnel feeding visible wins.

Bottom line

Build cost is small. Operating cost is invisible against revenue. Time-to-live is ten weeks. Iwan's time is 30 minutes of setup and 15 minutes a week thereafter. The upside is a doubling or tripling of ARR within twelve months and a structural shift in what kind of business Bureau Intake becomes. The downside, if the inbound channel underperforms, is still a meaningful improvement in retention and pricing power on the existing client base.

The decision is whether Bureau Intake wants to remain a one-person service agency competing on price, or become the company that owns the measurement standard for Dutch physiotherapy local visibility. Both are valid. Only one is hard to copy.
